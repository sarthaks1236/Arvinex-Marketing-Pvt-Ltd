Website vs. Social Media: Where Should You Invest First?

Home / Blog / Website vs. Social Media
← Back to Blog
📅 July 2026 · ⏱ 4 min read · Arvinex Team

One of the most common questions we hear from new business owners is: "Should I build a website first, or focus on social media?" The honest answer is — it depends on what job you need your online presence to do. Here's the framework we walk every client through.

Start With Your Goal, Not the Platform

A website and a social media page solve different problems. A website is where people go to verify you're legitimate, see your pricing or services, and take action — call, message, or fill a form. Social media is where people discover you, get reminded you exist, and see proof that you're active and trustworthy.

When a Website Should Come First

  • You run Google Ads or want to show up in Google Search results
  • Customers need to see pricing, services, or a portfolio before contacting you
  • You want a professional first impression for B2B clients
  • You need a place to send traffic from WhatsApp, business cards, or ads

When Social Media Should Come First

  • Your product is highly visual (food, fashion, interiors, events)
  • Your audience is younger and discovers brands through Instagram or Facebook
  • You want to build relationships and trust before asking for a sale

The Honest Answer: Most Businesses Need Both — In Sequence

In our experience, the businesses that grow fastest treat their website as the "home base" and social media as the traffic driver that brings people to it. Start with a simple, professional website that clearly explains what you do and how to contact you. Then use social media consistently to bring warm traffic to that website.

If budget is tight, start with a lean one-page website and a consistent posting schedule on one platform — not five. Consistency on one channel beats being scattered across many.
