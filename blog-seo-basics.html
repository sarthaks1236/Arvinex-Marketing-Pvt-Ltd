5 SEO Basics Every Small Business Should Get Right

Home / Blog / SEO Basics
← Back to Blog
📅 July 2026 · ⏱ 5 min read · Arvinex Team

SEO can feel overwhelming when every article throws a hundred tactics at you. In reality, most small businesses get 80% of the benefit from just five fundamentals, done properly and consistently. Here they are, in the order we tackle them for new clients.

1. Claim and Complete Your Google Business Profile

If you serve local customers, this is the single highest-leverage thing you can do. Add your correct address, phone number, business hours, photos and category. Businesses with a complete, active profile consistently show up more often in local "map pack" results than those with an incomplete or unclaimed listing.

2. Make Sure Google Can Actually Read Your Website

Your website needs clear page titles, one core keyword focus per page, and fast load times. A beautiful website that loads slowly or has no proper page titles will consistently underperform a simpler site that gets these basics right.

3. Write Content Around What Customers Actually Search

Instead of guessing, think about the exact phrases a customer would type — "logo design near me", "best digital marketing agency in [city]" — and make sure at least one page on your site directly answers that phrase.

4. Get a Few Genuine Reviews

Review count and quality directly influence how much Google trusts your business. Ask happy customers for a short, honest review rather than chasing volume — a handful of detailed, genuine reviews outperforms dozens of generic one-liners.

5. Keep an Eye on Mobile Experience

The majority of local searches happen on mobile. If your site is slow, hard to tap, or requires zooming to read, you'll lose visitors before they even see what you offer — regardless of how well you rank.

The Real Secret: Consistency

None of this is exotic. The businesses that win at SEO usually aren't doing anything secret — they're simply consistent about these fundamentals month after month, while competitors set it up once and forget about it.
